What is an ADU?

An Auxiliary Dwelling Unit (ADU) is a self-contained residential structure built on the same lot as a primary single-family home. It has its own entrance, living space, and full bathroom. ADUs go by many names: backyard cottage, in-law suite, carriage house, pool house, or guest house. What makes them distinct is that they sit on an already-occupied residential property and are fully permitted and legal.

It has recently become easier for single-family homeowners to add an ADU to their property. If you own your home and your lot, you likely qualify.

  • From contract signing to delivery is just 2 to 3 months. Installation itself can be as short as 2 to 4 weeks.
